BIRYANI:

  1. Biryani is my all-time favorite dish because of its irresistible blend of flavors and aromatic spices.
  2. The tender, marinated meat or vegetables combined with fragrant basmati rice make biryani a true culinary deligh
  3. Whether it's chicken, mutton, or vegetable biryani, each variation has its unique charm that I can't resist.
  4. The saffron-infused rice and the garnish of fried onions give biryani its signature visual appeal and taste.
  5. Every bite of biryani is like a celebration of taste, and it's a dish that always leaves me craving for more!

RECIPE OF BIRYANI

A basic recipe for chicken biryani

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) Salt to taste Water for cooking rice

INSTRUCTION:

  • Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  • Heat oil and ghee in a large pot or deep pan. Add cumin seeds, cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  • Add the sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are no longer pink. Stir in the biryani masala powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the chicken is partially cooked
  • Add the chopped tomatoes, yogurt, and half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil begins to separate.
  • In a large pot, bring enough water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice. Cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked. Drain the rice.
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil and place it on a low flame. Cook for 20-25 minutes, allowing the biryani to steam and the rice to fully cook
  • Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture in the pot. Sprinkle the remaining coriander and mint leaves on top.
  • Once done, remove from heat and let it sit for a few minutes before opening the lid. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
  • Your delicious Chicken Biryani is ready to be served! You can garnish it with fried onions, boiled eggs, and serve it with raita or salad. Enjoy!

RECIPE OF KABLI PULAO

A basic recipe for kabli pulao, a delightful Afghan rice dish:

INGREDIENTS:

FOR THE RICE

  • 2 cups long-grain Basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

FOR THE CHICKEN (OR LAMB) MARINADE:

  • 500g chicken (or lamb) piece
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

FOR THE GARNISH:

  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)

INSTRUCTION:

  • Start by marinating the chicken (or lamb): In a bowl, combine the yogurt, vegetable oil, chopped onion, minced garlic, ginger paste, ground coriander, ground cumin, turmeric powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well. Add the chicken (or lamb) pieces, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
  • Rinse the Basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  • In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and the drained rice. Cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked (still slightly firm). Drain the rice and set it aside.
  • In a separate large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat 1/4 cup of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the thinly sliced onion and cook until it turns golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set them aside for garnish.
  • Add the marinated chicken (or lamb) to the pot with the remaining fried onions. Cook until the meat is browned on all side
  • Add the partially cooked rice to the pot with the meat. Gently mix to combine.
  • Reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and let the rice and meat cook together for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the meat is tender.
  • While the pulao is cooking, in a separate small pan, heat 1/4 cup of vegetable oil. Add the slivered almonds and raisins (if using) and fry until they turn golden brown. Remove from heat.
  • Once the pulao is done, remove it from the heat. Sprinkle the fried almonds and raisins over the top, and garnish with the reserved crispy fried onions.
  • Serve your delicious Kabuli Pulao hot with some cucumber and tomato salad or yogurt on the side. Enjoy your Afghan feast!

RECIPE OF BANNU PULAO:

Bannu Pulao is a flavorful Pakistani rice dish.

INGREDIENTS:

FOR THE RICE:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

    • FOR THE PULAO:

  • 500g boneless chicken or beef, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups thinly sliced onions
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish

INSTRUCTION:

  • Rinse the Basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  • In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and the drained rice. Cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked (still slightly firm). Drain the rice and set it aside
  • In a separate large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set them aside for garnish.
  • Add the cumin seeds to the remaining onions in the pot and sauté for a minute until fragrant
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add the chicken or beef pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides
  • Stir in the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a few minute
  • Add the plain yogurt and green chilies. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the meat to absorb the flavors and become tender.
